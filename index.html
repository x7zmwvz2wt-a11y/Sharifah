🌌 Black Holes

What is a Black Hole?

A black hole is one of the most mysterious and powerful objects in the universe. It forms when a massive star collapses under its own gravity at the end of its life cycle.

The gravitational pull is so strong that not even light can escape, making black holes completely invisible.

At the center lies the singularity, surrounded by the event horizon. Beyond this boundary, nothing can return.

Black holes help scientists understand space, time, and the structure of galaxies.

Types

  • Stellar
  • Supermassive
  • Intermediate
